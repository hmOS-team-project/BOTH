# Bayesian Online Learning for Automated Team Coordination in Human-Machine Partnerships
Human-machine teams are increasingly utilized in industrial settings to enhance efficiency and reduce expenses. Effective Task Scheduling and Allocation (TSA) strategies are crucial for optimizing team performance by aligning tasks with member capabilities and meeting temporal constraints. We propose a novel approach that formulates the TSA problem as a temporal-constrained contextual decision-making process, employing a fully adaptive coordination method based on Bayesian online learning, which outperforms traditional methods across various problem scales, as demonstrated in experiments conducted in a virtual multi-round scheduling environment.
